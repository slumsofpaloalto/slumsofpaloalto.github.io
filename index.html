How to succeed
through relentless failure

Silicon Valley, the real story

The story of a family that has weathered a series of ten startups and the environment that is the real “Shark Tank”: Silicon Valley.

  • Read the Kindle Version

A place where some people are very badly off, others struggle to keep afloat, but the news continuously reports on the minority of winners, even glossing over these winners’ dark sides and failures.

  • Read the B&W Print Version

An insider view on a series of ten startups, with no unicorns, and the lessons learned from their similarities and differences. Two daughters raised and educated within the tank by leveraging communities within Palo Alto that most people have never heard of.

Relentless failing at work and in life, relentless financial struggle, and how to progress through it all… because there is no other choice. Family, community, and commitment come first: they must succeed

Fuller summary

Approximately two hundred pages with two dozen illustrations and photographs.

The main throughline is about startups, what they do right and wrong, and how to make them work.

But the core of the book is about acknowledging and accepting failure, even embracing it, to enable you to succeed. Without failure there is no progress or real success.

Other throughlines include the family, community, gender bigotry, programming, social responsibility, education, and the tradeoffs of living in Silicon Valley.
